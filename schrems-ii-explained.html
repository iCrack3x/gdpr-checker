What is Schrems II?

Schrems II is a landmark decision by the Court of Justice of the European Union (CJEU) on July 16, 2020. Named after Austrian privacy activist Max Schrems, the ruling invalidated the EU-US Privacy Shield framework and imposed strict requirements on data transfers to the United States.

The Timeline

2011

Max Schrems files complaints against Facebook with the Irish Data Protection Commissioner regarding EU-US data transfers.

2015

Schrems I: CJEU invalidates Safe Harbor agreement, ruling that US surveillance laws violate EU fundamental rights.

2016

EU and US negotiate Privacy Shield as Safe Harbor replacement.

July 16, 2020

Schrems II: CJEU invalidates Privacy Shield, ruling that US surveillance laws (FISA 702) still don't meet EU standards.

2023-2026

Businesses scramble to implement Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) with additional safeguards. New EU-US Data Privacy Framework emerges.

Why the US Failed the Test

The CJEU identified fundamental problems with US surveillance law:

  • Section 702 FISA: Allows US intelligence to collect foreign communications without individual warrants
  • Executive Order 12333: Permits mass surveillance outside US borders
  • No Redress: EU citizens cannot effectively challenge surveillance in US courts
  • Proportionality Issues: US surveillance is not limited to what's strictly necessary
Key Quote from the Ruling: "The limitations on the protection of personal data arising from US law are not circumscribed in a way that satisfies requirements of EU law."

What Businesses Must Do Now

1. Data Transfer Impact Assessment (DTIA)

Before transferring data to the US, you must:

  • Map all data flows to third countries
  • Assess surveillance risks in destination countries
  • Evaluate if supplementary measures are needed

2. Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)

The CJEU upheld SCCs BUT with conditions:

  • Verify if SCCs provide effective protection in practice
  • Implement supplementary technical measures if needed
  • Be prepared to suspend transfers if protection is inadequate

3. Supplementary Measures

When SCCs alone aren't enough:

Measure Effectiveness Use Case
End-to-end encryption High Emails, file transfers
Pseudonymization Medium Analytics, databases
EU-only processing Highest Sensitive personal data
